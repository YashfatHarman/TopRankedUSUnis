List of colleges and universities in Idaho

This is a list of colleges and universities in Idaho. This list also includes other educational institutions providing higher education, meaning tertiary, quaternary, and, in some cases, post-secondary education.

State institutions[edit]

Highest Degree Earned: Associate's or Certificate (average length of program)[edit]

  • College of Southern Idaho, (3 years), Twin Falls
  • College of Western Idaho, (3 years), Nampa, Idaho
  • Eastern Idaho Technical College, (no information), Idaho Falls
  • North Idaho College, Coeur d'Alene, (3 years)

Highest Degree Earned: Bachelor's/Master's (average length of program)[edit]

  • Boise State University, research university in Boise (6.2 years/Bachelor's - 2.7 years/Master's.)
  • Idaho State University, research university in Pocatello (5.8 years/Bachelor's - 2.4 years/Master's.)
  • Lewis-Clark State College, baccalaureate university in Lewiston (5 years/Bachelor's - no degrees available /Master's.)
  • University of Idaho, research university in Moscow (5.2 years/Bachelor's - 2.7 years/Master's.)

Private institutions[edit]

  • Boise Bible College, Bible college affiliated with the Churches of Christ in Boise
  • Brigham Young University-Idaho, baccalaureate university affiliated with the LDS Church in Rexburg
  • The College of Idaho, nonsectarian baccalaureate university in Caldwell
  • New Saint Andrews College, classical Christian institution affiliated with the Communion of Reformed Evangelical Churches in Moscow
  • Northwest Nazarene University, master's university affiliated with the Church of the Nazarene in Nampa
  • Stevens–Henager College, career college based in Utah with campuses in Idaho Falls and Boise
  • McCall College, community-based college in McCall and Boise.
