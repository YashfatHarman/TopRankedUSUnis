This is a list of colleges and universities in Alaska. This list also includes other educational institutions providing higher education, meaning tertiary, quaternary, and, in some cases, post-secondary education.

State institutions[edit]

Two-year institutions[edit]

  • Ilisagvik College, tribal community college in Barrow
  • Kodiak College, community college in Kodiak
  • Kenai Peninsula College,[1] community college with campuses in Soldotna, Homer, Seward
  • Matanuska-Susitna College, community college in Palmer
  • Prince William Sound College, community college in Valdez

Four-year institutions[edit]

  • University of Alaska System
    • University of Alaska Anchorage, doctoral university in Anchorage
    • University of Alaska Fairbanks, doctoral university in Fairbanks
    • University of Alaska Southeast, master's university with campuses in Juneau (main campus), Ketchikan, Sitka

Private institutions[edit]

Two-year institutions[edit]

  • Charter College,[2] for-profit associate's college in Anchorage

Four-year institutions[edit]

  • Alaska Bible College, Nondenominational Bible college in Glennallen
  • Alaska Pacific University, baccalaureate university associated with the United Methodist Church in Anchorage

Graduate institutions[edit]

  • Saint Herman's Orthodox Theological Seminary, seminary of the Orthodox Church in America in Kodiak

Notes[edit]

  1. ^ KPC offers a limited number of four-year degrees.
  2. ^ Charter College also offers several four-year programs.
